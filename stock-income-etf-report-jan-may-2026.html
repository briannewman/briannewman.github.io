Takeaways

GOOG vs. GOOW

GOOG returned about +19.45% including its March dividend. GOOW rose only +5.83% on price, but $10.33084 in distributions lifted cash total return to +20.30%.

GOOW narrowly wins this period. Its result reflects WeeklyPay structure, leveraged weekly exposure, distributions, path dependency, and return of capital risk.

TSLA vs. TSLW

TSLA was nearly flat at -0.52%. TSLW fell -19.08% on price, and its $4.88934 in distributions reduced the loss to -3.96%.

TSLW helped versus its own price decline but still trailed TSLA on total return.

NVDA vs. NVDW

NVDA returned +11.81% including its small March dividend. NVDW's weekly distributions lifted its return to +10.38% despite a -6.47% price decline.

NVDW kept the gap close, but raw NVDA slightly led for the period.

COIN vs. COIW

COIN fell -20.08%. COIW fell much more on price, but $3.87253 in distributions reduced its cash total return to -24.81%.

Raw COIN won this pair, though both sides were hurt by the underlying drawdown.

PLTR vs. PLTW

PLTR declined -6.74%. PLTW distributions reduced a steep price loss, but cash total return was still -12.35%.

Raw PLTR won this pair because the distribution stream did not fully offset PLTW's price decline.

GDX vs. GDXW

GDX gained +4.39%. GDXW fell -18.16% on price, but $12.65692 in distributions brought cash total return back to +3.71%.

GDXW nearly matched GDX, but the raw gold miners ETF finished ahead.

YMAG

YMAG declined -8.64% on price, but $2.10180 in distributions lifted cash total return to +6.25%.

This is diversified YieldMax option-income fund-of-funds exposure rather than raw Magnificent 7 equity exposure.

YQQQ

YQQQ declined -18.06% on price. Distributions reduced the loss, but cash total return remained negative at -7.65%.

That result fits a strong tech tape because YQQQ is short Nasdaq-100-oriented.

CHPY

CHPY was the standout at +59.11% cash total return, combining a +40.17% price gain with $10.92530 in distributions.

Its result dominated the expanded set.

MAGC and OZEM

MAGC declined -23.42% and OZEM declined -7.51%. Neither had an in-period distribution by ex-date.

These two behaved like straight price-return rows during this window.

Conclusion

Using the tickers provided, this analysis compares raw equity exposure, Roundhill WeeklyPay leveraged distribution ETFs, YieldMax income funds, and several standalone thematic ETFs.

For the six direct pairs, raw exposure won five: TSLA, NVDA, COIN, PLTR, and GDX. Distribution ETF exposure won one: GOOW. CHPY was the strongest standalone result, while COIW and MAGC were the weakest cash-return rows in the expanded set.
