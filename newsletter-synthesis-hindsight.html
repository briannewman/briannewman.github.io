What Counts As Hindsight

A claim ages well when later articles clarify the mechanism it named, make its tension more visible, or show that the synthesis framed a durable structure rather than a temporary story.

A claim does not age well merely because a later article uses overlapping words. The lexical match is a lead for review, not a proof of accuracy.

What Is Not Published Here

The public page intentionally omits raw claim packets, article IDs, corpus excerpts, and gated-source text. Those artifacts remain local review material.

The next step is a judge pass that classifies each claim as aged well, partly right, contradicted, too vague, or not hindsight-testable.

The reviewer should grade claim survival, not original model victory. The useful classifications are aged well, partly right, contradicted, too vague, and not hindsight-testable.

The output should identify best-aged claims, worst-aged claims, missed future developments, and prompt-design lessons.

Interpretation Guardrail

High future candidate rates are expected for broad AI and market claims. They are useful for routing attention, but they should not become a leaderboard or automatic score.

The real judgment is whether later evidence made the old synthesis more precise, less precise, or obsolete.
