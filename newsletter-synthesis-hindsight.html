What Counts As Hindsight

A claim ages well when later articles clarify the mechanism it named, make its tension more visible, or show that the synthesis framed a durable structure rather than a temporary story.

A claim does not age well merely because a later article uses overlapping words. The lexical match is a lead for review, not a proof of accuracy.

Cross-Run Result

Mechanism-bearing claims aged best: governed agency, orchestration, memory, permissions, tool contracts, auditability, inference infrastructure, power, routing, financing, and bottleneck ownership.

Broad legitimacy, resilience, crisis-escalation, and institutional-decay abstractions aged worse unless they named a concrete mechanism and observable implication.

Prompt Lesson

Future syntheses should preserve claim-level source handles and label each unit as mechanism, tension, absence, prediction, question, or analogy. Those categories age differently.

Risk scenarios need trigger, mechanism, expected observable, and time horizon. Otherwise live crisis temperature masquerades as structure.
