What changed versus 2026

The early-2025 window was much less favorable for the weekly option-income wrappers. PLTR and GDX were strong raw winners, while TSLA, NVDA, COIN, and PLTR all beat their available Roundhill counterparts on the cash-return method.

The option ETFs still produced large cash distributions, but those payments did not fully offset price decay in TSLW, NVDW, and COIW. PLTW had a positive cash return, but it was overwhelmed by PLTR's raw equity rally.

Coverage caveats

GOOW and GDXW did not exist during the Jan. 1-May 29, 2025 window. TSLW, NVDW, COIW, and PLTW are partial-period from Feb. 19, 2025. CHPY is partial-period from Apr. 8, 2025.

Current tickers are used throughout. In the 2025 trading window, TSLW and NVDW corresponded to earlier Roundhill tickers TSW and NVW, while MAGC traded as the renamed China Dragons strategy later in 2025.

Pair Takeaways

The pair score excludes GOOG/GOOW and GDX/GDXW because the income ETF side had no in-window trading data.

GOOG vs. GOOW

GOOG finished down 9.16% on the cash-return method, helped only slightly by a $0.20 dividend. GOOW launched after the period, so there is no valid Jan.-May 2025 counterpart comparison.

TSLA vs. TSLW

TSLA lost 5.50%. TSLW produced $5.27 of distributions from Mar. 3 through May 27, but its partial-period cash return was still -7.31% after the share price fell from $51.35 to $42.33.

NVDA vs. NVDW

NVDA was almost flat but slightly positive at +0.64%. NVDW paid $4.42 in distributions, yet the partial-period cash return was -4.82% because the ETF price dropped from $49.89 to $43.06.

COIN vs. COIW

COIN declined 3.25%. COIW's high distributions, totaling $5.12, were not enough to offset its deeper price slide, leaving a partial-period cash return of -11.32%.

PLTR vs. PLTW

PLTR was the runaway winner at +62.68%. PLTW still managed a positive +4.02% cash return after $5.72 in distributions, but it captured only a small fraction of the raw equity move.

GDX vs. GDXW

GDX rose 42.28% with no in-window distribution. GDXW launched after the cutoff, so there is no valid 2025 counterpart comparison.
